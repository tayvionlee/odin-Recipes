Date Night Shrimp Francese over Angel Hair Pasta

This elegant dinner is a fabulous dinner to serve for date night at home, for a dinner party, and even if you're doing dinner solo it's still an awesome meal to enjoy.

Comes together in less than 30 minutes

  • Prep: 10 mins
  • Cook: 20 mins
  • Total: 30 mins
  • Servings: 4
  • Yield: 4 servings

Ingredients:

  • 1 (12 ounce) package angel hair pasta
  • 1 pound uncooked medium shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • ¾ cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon granulated garlic
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • ¼ cup grapeseed oil, divided
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, divided
  • 1 ¼ cups chicken broth
  • ¼ cup dry white wine, such as Pinot Grigio
  • ¼ cup whipping cream
  • 1 ½ tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley (optional)
    •

Directions

    Step 1

  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook angel hair pasta in the boiling water, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite, 4 to 5 minutes. Drain.

    2. Step 2

  2. Meanwhile, butterfly shrimp, and lightly press open.

    3. Step 3

  3. Mix flour, salt, garlic, and black pepper in a shallow dish. Reserve 2 tablespoons flour mixture and set aside. Dredge shrimp in flour mixture until well coated, shaking off excess flour.

    4. Step 4

  4. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Add 2 tablespoons oil and allow to get hot. Melt 2 tablespoons butter in the hot oil, add shrimp, and cook until shrimp are bright pink on the outside and the meat is opaque, 3 to 5 minutes. Transfer shrimp to a pan lined with a drip rack and keep warm.

    5. Step 5

  5. Heat remaining 2 tablespoons oil in the same skillet and stir in reserved flour mixture. Stir and cook for 1 to 2 minutes, but be careful so it doesn't burn. Add chicken broth and white wine. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, reduce heat, and add cream and lemon juice. Stir and cook until sauce has slightly thickened, 3 to 5 minutes. Remove skillet from heat and stir in remaining 2 tablespoons butter until melted.

    6. Step 6

  6. Divide pasta into serving bowls, top with lemon sauce, and 4 to 5 shrimp per serving. Garnish with chopped parsley.

Banana Bread

  • Prep: 15 mins
  • Cook: 1 hr
  • Total: 1 hr 15 mins
  • Servings 16
  • Yield: 2 7x3-inch loaves

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • ¼ cup butter, melted
  • 3 bananas, mashed

Directions

    Step 1:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease and flour two 7x3-inch loaf pans.

    2. Step 2:

  2. In one bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, salt, and sugar. Mix in slightly beaten eggs, melted butter, and mashed bananas. Stir in nuts if desired. Pour into the prepared pans.

    3. Step 3:

  3. Bake in the preheated oven for 1 hour, or until a wooden toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.

Baked Old Bay® Salmon Baked Old Bay® Salmon

  • Prep: 5 mins
  • Cook: 20 mins
  • Total: 25 mins
  • Servings: 8
  • Yield: 8 Servings
    •

Ingredients

  • 1 serving cooking spray
  • 1 (2 pound) salmon fillet
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons seafood seasoning (such as Old Bay®)

Directions

    Step 1:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Line a large baking sheet with aluminum foil and spray with cooking spray.

    2. Step 2:

  2. Place salmon on the prepared baking sheet. Spread with mayonnaise and sprinkle with Old Bay® seasoning.

    3. Step 3:

  3. Bake in preheated oven until fish flakes easily with a fork, about 20 minutes.
